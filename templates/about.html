Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS)

WHAT IS PCOS?

PCOS is a common health problem caused by an imbalance of reproductive hormones. Women with PCOS produce higher-than-normal amounts of male hormones. The hormonal imbalance creates problems in the ovaries. The ovaries make the egg that is released each month as part of a healthy menstrual cycle. With PCOS, the egg may not develop as it should or it may not be released during ovulation as it should be, causing their body to skip menstrual periods and making harder for them to get pregnant. The ovaries may develop numerous small collections of fluid (follicles) and fail to regularly release eggs.


SYMPTOMS OF PCOS

Some of the symptoms of PCOS include:

Irregular menstrual cycle: One may miss periods or have fewer periods or periods may come every 21 days or more often. Some may even stop menstruating

Too much hair:Excess hair on the face, chin, or parts of the body where men usually have hair. This is called "hirsutism." Hirsutism affects up to 70% of women with PCOS.

Acne: Pimples/acne on the face, chest, and upper back

Thinning hair Excess hair loss on the scalp or male-pattern baldness

Weight gain: Gaining excess weight difficulty losing weight

Darkening of skin: Particularly along neck creases, in the groin, and underneath breasts

Skin tags: Small excess flaps of skin in the armpits or neck area

TIPS TO MANAGE PCOS

1. Maintain a healthy weight
Even a modest weight reduction (5-10%) can significantly improve PCOS symptoms by balancing hormones, enhancing insulin sensitivity, and regulating menstrual cycles. Aim for gradual changes in diet and activity for sustainable results.

2. Limit Carbohydrates
Reducing carbohydrate intake helps control insulin levels, supporting hormone regulation and weight management. Choose complex carbs (whole grains, veggies) over refined ones to maintain stable blood sugar and reduce cravings.

3. Be Active
Regular exercise improves insulin sensitivity and supports weight goals. Aim for at least 30 minutes of enjoyable physical activity most days, whether itâ€™s brisk walking, strength training, or yoga.

4. Eat a Balanced, Anti-Inflammatory Diet
Focus on high-fiber foods, lean proteins, healthy fats, and anti-inflammatory spices like turmeric and ginger. This balanced approach helps manage insulin and reduce inflammation, key factors in PCOS management.
Maintain healthy diet with high-fiber vegetables, lean protein foods and anti-inflammatory foods and spices

5. Prioritize Stress Management and Sleep
High stress can worsen PCOS symptoms, so incorporate relaxation practices and aim for 7-8 hours of quality sleep. Lower stress and adequate rest can help stabilize hormones and improve overall well-being.
